The Centre

The Centre for Excellence in Camel Research & Development (CE-CaRD) at the Faculty of Veterinary Medicine & Animal Husbandry, Somali National University, advances camel science for safer milk, healthier herds, and climate-resilient rangelands in Somalia and the Horn of Africa.

  • Genetics & Improvement
  • Milk Quality & Safety
  • Health & AMR
  • Welfare & Non-invasive Phenotyping
  • Rangelands & Climate
  • Value Chains & Policy

Mission

Generate decision-ready evidence, build local skills, and co-create practical solutions with producers and partners so Somalia’s camel sector delivers safe milk, healthy herds, resilient rangelands, and fair livelihoods across the Horn of Africa.

Vision

Somalia leads the region in camel science and practice with healthy camels, safe and high-quality camel products, climate-smart rangelands, and thriving pastoral communities supported by world-class Somali research and partnerships.

What we do

  • Research: genetics; milk quality & safety; health/AMR; welfare/phenotyping; rangelands; value chains.
  • Training: short courses; student supervision; on-farm skills.
  • Services: standardized measurements; data methods; practical guidance.

Impact highlights

  • Somali camel ecotype baselines (morphometrics; udder/teat/milk vein metrics).
  • Five-year milk recording informing yield, seasonality, and management signals.
  • Ongoing: non-invasive phenotyping (SNU + IFAPA), value chain mapping, CAMEL-SHIELD GWAS.

Partners

ILRI • University of Bari • University of Sydney • University of Adelaide • IFAPA (Spain) • Somali producer partners (Beder, Milic, Aaran)
