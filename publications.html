  1. Gross and Histopathological Lesions Associated with Platyhelminth Infections in Camels (Camelus dromedarius) at a Somali Meat Processing Facility

    2025 Journal article

    Insights in Animal Science (2025). DOI: 10.69917/ias.02.01-05

    Summary

    Pathology from an abattoir series documented bile-duct changes, granulomas, and necrosis consistent with trematode/cestode burdens.

  2. A Study on Gross and Histopathological Lesions of Platyhelminthes in Liver from Slaughtered Camel at Some-Meat Company, Mogadishu, Somalia

    2025 Preprint

    Preprints.org (2025, preprint). DOI: 10.20944/preprints202501.0446.v1

    Summary

    Reports 84.5% livers with platyhelminth-associated lesions; details gross/microscopic patterns (e.g., infiltration, granulomas, bile-duct changes).

  3. Prevalence of Mastitis and Associated Risk Factors in Lactating Camels (Camelus dromedarius) on Dairy Farms in Benadir Region, Somalia

    2024 Journal article

    Open Journal of Veterinary Medicine (2024). DOI: 10.4236/ojvm.2024.146008

    Summary

    CMT study (96 camels; 384 quarters) found 34.4% animal-level and 46.3% quarter-level mastitis; risks linked to early lactation, age, and parity.

  4. Knowledge, Attitude, and Practice Related to Antibiotic Use and Resistance among Dairy Camel Farmers in Benadir, Somalia

    2024 Journal article

    ARC Journal of Animal and Veterinary Sciences (2024). DOI: 10.20431/2455-2518.0901004

    Summary

    KAP survey showed limited awareness of withdrawal periods and low veterinary oversight, highlighting stewardship gaps on camel dairies.

  5. Major Constraints of Intensification of Camel Husbandry in Mogadishu, Somalia

    2023 Journal article

    Animal and Veterinary Sciences (2023). DOI: 10.11648/j.avs.20231103.14

    Summary

    Survey of 67 farms identified feed cost, knowledge gaps, disease, and markets as key intensification barriers; mean daily milk ≈4 L/camel.

  6. Prevalence and Risk Factors Associated with Dermatophytosis in One-Humped Camels in Benadir, Somalia

    2022 Journal article

    Global Journal of Medical Research (2022). DOI not listed by the journal.

    Summary

    Cross-sectional screening (n=384) reported 7.5% KOH-confirmed ringworm; highest in camels <3 years.

  7. Antibiotic Residue in Raw Milk Collected from Dairy Farms and Markets in Benadir, Somalia

    2020 Journal article

    PAMJ – One Health (2020). DOI: 10.11604/pamj-oh.2020.2.19.24814

    Summary

    Cross-sectional survey (n=100) found 24% of raw milk samples positive on Delvotest, indicating residue risks in Benadir supply chains.
